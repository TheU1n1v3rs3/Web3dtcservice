Decentralized Hosting Technologies

IPFS (InterPlanetary File System)

A distributed file system that creates a permanent and decentralized method of storing and sharing files.

Key Features:

  • Content-addressed storage
  • Automatic deduplication
  • Peer-to-peer distribution
  • Version control
  • Offline accessibility

Best For:

Static websites, blogs, documentation, and content that needs global distribution without central servers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can my censorship-resistant website be taken down?

No single entity can take down a properly deployed censorship-resistant website. It's distributed across thousands of nodes worldwide, making censorship practically impossible.

How fast are decentralized websites?

Modern decentralized networks can be as fast as traditional hosting, especially with proper optimization and CDN integration through public gateways.

What happens if IPFS goes down?

IPFS is a decentralized network that can't "go down" completely. Even if some nodes fail, your content remains accessible through other nodes and backup storage on Arweave.

Can I update my decentralized website?

Yes, you can update your website by deploying new versions and updating the IPFS hash linked to your domain. We provide update management as part of our service.
